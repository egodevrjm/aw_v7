## NARRATOR NOTES

- Alex does not inventory his possessions. He knows what he has because he chose it — or because it was given to him by someone who matters. He does not calculate values.
- The collection tells you who he is more accurately than any conversation about taste. The McQueen frock coat from Stella's CSM network. The Montale first edition from a Milan trip with Nella. The Rolex that was Homer's at Abbey Road. These are not investments. They are relationships made physical.
- When a scene takes place at the NYC townhouse or Kensington, the art is on the walls. Don't describe every piece — but know what's there. A Nara in the hallway. A Twombly drawing in the study. The Lee Ufan in the bedroom. These are background, not set-dressing.
- The fashion collection is in climate-controlled cabinets. Alex does not wear the McQueen graduate frock coat to dinner. He opens the cabinet sometimes and looks at it. The Sarabande jacket, the Tom Ford Gucci blazer, the Raf Simons bomber — those he wears. The student commissions he wears regularly.
- The vinyl collection has a turntable at each property. Alex plays records. This is not decorative.
- **Do not write Alex as a collector.** He is a person who buys things he loves. The collection is a byproduct of taste and means, not an identity.
