## CODY ASHFORD
person_id: cody_ashford
syntax: multi-text bursts; ALL-CAPS for emphasis; runs hot on enthusiasm
pacing: fast; impatient; launches plans before they're formed
taboo: restraint; quiet; picking a reasonable option when an unreasonable one exists
samples:
  - "OK HEAR ME OUT"
  - "I'M TELLING YOU RIGHT NOW this is going to be the best night of our lives"
  - "tommy's wrong. i'm not wrong. alex back me up"
  - "wait wait wait wait what if we"
  - [three messages in 45 seconds]: "also" / "also" / "actually never mind"
SB role: high-volume catalyst; the proposal engine; gets shot down regularly; keeps proposing
narrator note: Cody is not stupid; he's enthusiastic; the jokes land because he's self-aware about the ratio of his proposals to their success rate

## JAKE PATTERSON
person_id: jake_patterson
syntax: minimal clauses; logistics-first; often fewer than eight words
pacing: posts rarely; says the thing that needs saying then stops
taboo: over-explaining; speculating beyond available information; Cody-level enthusiasm
samples:
  - "i'll drive."
  - "weather looks bad saturday"
  - "horse is fine. false alarm."
  - "what time?"
  - [read receipt; no response] (the silence is meaningful; Jake has logged the information)
SB role: grounded logistics; horses/land/transport; the practical anchor that makes plans actually happen
narrator note: Jake is the group's most reliable actual data source; when Jake says something will work it usually does; when Jake says nothing, the plan was probably fine

## DANNY HARLAN
person_id: danny_harlan
syntax: clean full sentences; dry irony; arrives after reading the whole thread
pacing: posts after everyone else; the thread has run its course; Danny lands the line
taboo: rushing; arriving first; enthusiasm that isn't precisely calibrated
samples:
  - "fascinating."
  - "that should go well."
  - "I've read this entire thread and I have one question: why?"
  - "Cody's plan is wrong in several specific ways I'll explain to anyone who asks."
  - "I'm in." (said about a bad idea; the confidence is part of the joke)
SB role: late precision; the group's editorial voice; delivers the understated line that makes everyone stop scrolling and laugh
narrator note: Danny is the observer; he lives one beat behind the scene and uses that beat to say the thing everyone was thinking but hadn't found yet; he is rarely wrong

## SB CHAT TEXTURE NOTES

The SB chat runs daily. It carried closeness through six years of distance. Most messages are mundane: sports results, weather, work days, weekend logistics, the twenty-bucks joke. The chat is not a comedy show. It's real people maintaining a real friendship across a weird situation. The jokes are good because the underlying warmth is real.

Rendering rules:
- Not every message is funny. Most messages are "ok" / "what time" / "later"
- The running jokes (not Cody, the twenty bucks, Kayla's crush on Alex) appear sparingly — once or twice a scene, never every message
- Jake's logistics and Danny's precision are most effective when the thread has run a while first
- Alex in this chat sounds more Kentucky than anywhere else; he adjusts toward his oldest friends without noticing
