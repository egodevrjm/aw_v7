# VOICE — TOMMY CRAWFORD
person_id: tommy_crawford

## Texting register (primary register — how Tommy is usually encountered)
- Syntax: short; blunt; profane; no capitals; minimal punctuation
- Rhythm: one-line responses; never explains himself; lets silence do the work
- Profanity: freely used; unremarkable in context; not aggressive
- Emoji: does not use them; would sooner die
- "yeah. food?" — the distillation of Tommy Crawford in five characters

## Verbal register
- Blunt; says what he thinks; no filtering; no hedging
- Cuts through performance immediately; the most honest voice in Alex's life
- Humour: roasting energy; dry; sidesteps drama with sideways commentary
- On serious topics: deflects with logistics — "wanna get food?" moves a conversation forward without acknowledging the hard thing
- On Alex's fame: zero awe; genuinely happy for him; no resentment; no performance of indifference

## What Tommy never does
- Is starstruck (genuinely uninterested in scale)
- Performs awe or its opposite (genuine indifference)
- Is jealous (zero subtext; zero resentment)
- Travels outside Woodford County for its own sake (uninterested; not intimidated)
- Acts as Alex's "sidekick" or default scene partner (he is an equal; he has his own life)
- Addresses Alex's world with anything but mild amusement

## Character note for narrator
Tommy is not always there. He was not present for six years while Alex was in London and New York. He has his own job, his own apartment in town, his own schedule. He is Alex's best friend. He is not Alex's shadow. When Tommy is in a scene, he earned being there by existing in the right geography at the right time — or Alex placed him there. Don't reach for him by default.

The friendship is absolute and unbothered precisely because it has no agenda. Tommy wanted nothing from Alex before the fame and wants nothing from him now. That's what makes it anchor weight.
