> **What this is:** The complete post-Rule visual identity system for Alex's professional and personal life. Designed by Collins — the New York–based design agency — on commission through AL.X. This file is the single-source specification. When any scene involves stationery, letterhead, business cards, email signatures, envelopes, delivery notes, or printed ephemera from Alex's world, it should reflect this system. Do not invent alternative marks.

## THE FOUR MARKS

The Collins system resolves to four distinct marks, each with a specific use case. They are not interchangeable.

### 1. AL.X MARK (primary professional identity)

- **Use:** AL.X management entity — Latham, Priya, Oliver. Professional outbound. Business cards, letterhead, email signature for the London Mayfair office. Any communication *about* Alex rather than *from* Alex.
- **Form:** "AL.X" as a typographic mark — the period between "AL" and "X" is structural, not punctuation. Custom letterform derived from a neo-grotesque base. The period is a precise dot, proportioned deliberately.
- **Register:** Professional, present, unfussy. Not logo-y. Reads as infrastructure, not branding.
- **Colour:** Black on cream (primary) or cream on black (reverse). Gold foil reserved for specific applications (see §Gold Accents).

### 2. AW MONOGRAM (personal identity)

- **Use:** Alex's personal stationery. Thank-you cards, personal correspondence, private notes, gift tags, envelope closures. Anything *from Alex personally* rather than *from AL.X on Alex's behalf*.
- **Form:** A two-letter ligature — the A and W overlapping such that the W's centre valley aligns with the A's apex. Custom drawn by Collins. Serif-influenced but not serif; reads as old-money restraint, not heritage-mimicry.
- **Register:** Private, warm, considered. It is the mark that closes a handwritten note.
- **Colour:** Gold foil on cream is primary. Blind-deboss on cream for the most restrained applications (condolences, very private correspondence).

### 3. WALKER M (family / archival mark)

- **Use:** Family and archival references. Walker Holdings internal. The Archive (Homer's master tapes vault at Kensington). Anything that touches the multi-generational Walker legacy rather than Alex's individual professional life.
- **Form:** A stylised capital M — the Walker family mark Homer has used on album production credits since the 1970s. Collins preserved it unchanged; they did not redesign it. The decision was deliberate — the M is heritage.
- **Register:** Heritage, weight, lineage. Not current. Carries decades.
- **Colour:** Traditionally gold on cream, occasionally black on cream for archival paperwork.

### 4. (Implicit) WHITE-LABEL HATFIELD OVERLAY

- **Use:** Where Hatfield Group materials intersect Alex's world — e.g., The Stave NYC membership cards, Hatfield group correspondence to Alex.
- **Note:** This is NOT part of the Collins commission. Hatfield has its own identity system (older, established). Collins's mandate was Alex's and AL.X's identities only. Do not conflate.

## MATERIAL SPECIFICATIONS

### Paper / Stock
- **Primary stock:** Cream. Specific weight — **300gsm cotton, mould-made, cream tone** (not white, not ivory — cream, which sits between). Sourced from a small mill; Collins selected after a multi-week paper comparison.
- **Secondary stock:** Matte black 270gsm for envelope linings and the occasional reverse application.
- **Never use:** Pure white. Pure black. Glossy finishes. Coated stocks. Novelty textures. The paper decision is itself part of the identity.

### Ink / Foil
- **Primary ink:** Black (offset letterpress for business cards; flat offset for letterhead).
- **Gold accents:** **Restrained.** Gold appears only on the AW monogram (personal stationery) and the Walker M (family/archival). It does not appear on AL.X professional materials. The gold is a specific warm tone — not yellow, not rose — closer to an antique brass.
- **Never use:** Silver. Multiple accent colours. Holographic or shifting foils. Rainbow effects. Any maximalist decorative treatment.

### Typography
- **Custom AL.X typeface:** Designed by Collins specifically for the mark; used for the mark and limited supporting text only.
- **Secondary typeface (body text, letterhead addresses, business card secondary info):** A classically-proportioned serif — Collins selected a cut close to but not exactly Caslon, with a modernised aperture. Body weights only; no italics used decoratively.
- **Never use:** Sans-serif body text. Decorative fonts. Script fonts (except the handwritten signature on Alex's personal correspondence, which is Alex's actual handwriting, not a type).

## APPLICATIONS

### Business Cards — AL.X
- 3.5 × 2 inches (US standard). 450gsm cream stock (double-thickness). Blind-deboss AL.X mark on front. Name + role + contact on reverse in black offset. Edge-painted in matte black on the thickest applications. Produced by a small letterpress house in Brooklyn Collins contracts.

### Letterhead — AL.X
- A4 cream, 300gsm. AL.X mark top-centre, restrained scale. Mayfair London address + phone + email bottom-right in small serif. Nothing else.

### Personal Stationery — Alex
- A5 cream folded card, 300gsm. AW monogram embossed centre-front in gold foil. Interior blank. Envelopes match; AW monogram on the flap in blind-deboss (no foil on the envelope exterior — the recipient's address is the point). A smaller A6 correspondence card for shorter notes follows the same spec.

### Gift Tags / Closures
- Circular cream tag, 1.25" diameter, 300gsm. AW monogram in gold foil. Single punch hole, no string provided (the gifter ties it).

### Thank-You Cards
- Alex's primary volume production. He writes a lot of them. AW monogram, blind-deboss only (gold is reserved for the most personal correspondence — birthdays, closest people). Volume production is blind-deboss.

## NARRATOR NOTES

- **Never call it "branding."** The Collins work is identity, not branding. Branding is for products. This is for a person.
- **Never invent alternative marks.** No "AW cypher" variations, no decorative corner elements, no seals. If a scene needs a visual, it is one of the four marks above.
- **The gold is restrained.** Not every piece has gold. Most pieces are blind-deboss or black offset. Gold is the exception, reserved for the personal tier.
- **The paper matters.** When describing a card, note the cream (not white), the weight (it feels substantial), the deboss (you feel the mark with a fingertip before you see it).
- **The first two cards were private.** When writing the Collins rollout, the first public-adjacent moment is AL.X letterhead post-June-5, not the business cards or the personal stationery.
