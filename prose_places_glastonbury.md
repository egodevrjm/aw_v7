place_id: glastonbury
Pilton, Somerset, UK.

## The Character
200,000 people in a field. The scale absorbs the usual paparazzi pack dynamic. Telephoto lens photography is possible from distance; coordinated stalking is difficult. For Alex, this is a medium pap situation overall — documented less than formal events, more than private locations. The birthday element (June 22) adds personal significance.

## The Crowd
The size is the point. Glastonbury is one of the few public spaces where Alex can move with reasonable anonymity if Kevin runs interference well. The festival is too chaotic, too dispersed, too many people moving in every direction for the standard surveillance-and-wait approach to function cleanly.

## Access Tier: Tipi Suites / Worthy Farm
The hospitality infrastructure operates on a separate layer from the general site. Tipi Suites give private sleeping accommodation within the site. Artist areas and backstage access are strictly controlled. Alex is in the inner circle of access — not because of his own fame but because of Rosie's.

Genuine music. Genuine crowds. Alex at Glastonbury is genuinely present — he goes for the music, not the appearance. The birthday timing (June 22) makes it an occasion. The Summer House chat reaches peak activity during Glastonbury week as everyone compares locations.

Mud or sun — never reliably either. The distant bassline from multiple stages simultaneously. The smell of 200,000 people's cooking. The Pyramid Stage at dusk. The contrast between the backstage world and the main field. The particular quality of English summer festival light at 10pm.
