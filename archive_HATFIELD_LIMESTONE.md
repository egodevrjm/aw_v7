# LIMESTONE SPRINGS BEVERAGE CO.

---

## THE OPENING PRINCIPLE

**Limestone Springs is a premium soda, mixer, and sparkling water company built on the same Kentucky limestone geology that makes bourbon possible — and it has gone global.**

This is not bottled water with a nice label. Limestone Springs is a full beverage company producing a range of sodas, tonics, ginger beers, sparkling waters, and cocktail mixers distributed in over 30 countries. The brand proposition is simple and real: Kentucky's limestone-filtered water has a natural mineral profile that makes everything it touches taste better. Bourbon distillers have known this for centuries. Limestone Springs puts it in a bottle — then builds a world-class mixer and soda range on top of it.

The company anchors Hatfield Group's non-alcoholic beverage strategy. It's the product that sits on every Hatfield Bar & Grill table, behind every Stave bar, on every Hatfield Inn room service menu — and increasingly, in bars, restaurants, and retail shelves worldwide.

Alex probably doesn't know the full scale of this operation. He knows the water and the ginger beer (it's at the compound, it's at Hatfield Lexington). He has no idea it's a globally distributed beverage company.

## THE STORY

**2002:** Hatfield Group started bottling Kentucky limestone spring water as a complementary product — something to serve alongside bourbon at the distillery and the restaurants. Simple operation: source, bottle, distribute locally.

**2005-2014:** The water gained a regional following. Kentucky, Tennessee, Ohio, Indiana — restaurants and bars that took their water seriously started stocking Limestone Springs. The mineral profile (naturally filtered through 300M-year-old limestone, adding calcium and magnesium, removing iron) gave the water a distinctive clean, slightly sweet character. Bourbon bars particularly liked it: the same water that makes their bourbon, served at the table.

**2015 — The pivot:** David Buckner saw the opportunity. The premium mixer market was exploding globally — Fever-Tree had proven that people would pay for quality tonic, quality ginger beer, quality mixers. The cocktail renaissance meant bartenders cared about every ingredient, including what they mixed with. Limestone Springs had the source water, the brand, and the Hatfield connection. Buckner hired a beverage director, invested in production capability, and expanded from water into a full mixer and craft soda range.

**2018-present — Global expansion:** The mixer range launched into the UK first (natural synergy with The Stave London and the growing bourbon cocktail scene). Then EU, Japan, Australia. Airline contracts followed (premium cabins stock Limestone Springs tonic and sparkling). Retail distribution expanded globally. The brand now generates more revenue from mixers and sodas than from water.

## PRODUCT RANGE

### Sparkling & Still Water

**Limestone Springs Sparkling Water**
- Naturally sourced, carbonated at the facility. Fine bubble, clean minerality.
- Available in 330ml and 750ml glass bottles (on-trade), 500ml and 1L PET (retail).
- House water at all Hatfield properties worldwide.
- Retail: $2.50-4.00 (US) / £2.00-3.50 (UK)

**Limestone Springs Still Water**
- Same source, unfizzy. Clean, slightly sweet mineral profile.
- Available in 330ml and 750ml glass bottles (on-trade), 500ml and 1L PET (retail).
- Retail: $2.00-3.50 (US) / £1.80-3.00 (UK)

### Tonic Waters

**Limestone Springs Premium Tonic**
- The flagship mixer. Natural quinine, Kentucky limestone water, subtle citrus. Lighter, cleaner, less sweet than most premium tonics.
- Designed to complement bourbon cocktails specifically (most tonics are designed for gin). The mineral profile bridges bourbon's caramel-oak notes rather than fighting them.
- Available in 200ml glass bottles (bar standard), 500ml bottles (retail), 4-packs.
- Retail: $6-8 per 4-pack
- Direct competitor to Fever-Tree, Q Mixers, East Imperial in the premium tonic category.

**Limestone Springs Light Tonic**
- Lower sugar, lower calorie. Same water, same quinine, restrained sweetness.
- Growing category — health-conscious drinkers want premium tonic without the sugar.
- Available in same formats as Premium Tonic.

**Limestone Springs Elderflower Tonic**
- Elderflower botanical infusion. Floral, delicate. Strong in UK and European markets.
- Available in 200ml glass bottles.

**Limestone Springs Aromatic Tonic**
- Angostura-style aromatics built into the tonic. Pink-tinged. Designed for gin-and-tonic and bourbon highball.
- Available in 200ml glass bottles.

### Ginger Beer & Ginger Ale

**Limestone Springs Ginger Beer**
- Proper ginger beer — spicy, with real ginger root, not ginger flavouring. Noticeable heat. The mixer for Kentucky Mules (bourbon instead of vodka, Limestone Springs ginger beer, lime).
- This is the product bartenders request by name. The ginger beer is arguably the most celebrated product in the range.
- Available in 200ml glass bottles, 330ml cans, 4-packs.
- Retail: $6-8 per 4-pack

**Limestone Springs Ginger Ale**
- Lighter, sweeter, less spicy than the ginger beer. Traditional ginger ale for whiskey highballs and casual mixing.
- Available in 330ml cans and 500ml bottles.

### Craft Sodas

**Limestone Springs Cola**
- Craft cola made with real vanilla, kola nut extract, cinnamon, citrus oils. No high-fructose corn syrup. Darker, more complex than mass-market cola. Makes an exceptional bourbon-and-cola.
- Available in 330ml glass bottles and cans.
- Retail: $2.50-3.50 per bottle

**Limestone Springs Lemon**
- Clean lemon soda. Real lemon juice, light sweetness, high carbonation. An adult lemonade.
- Available in 330ml glass bottles and cans.

**Limestone Springs Orange**
- Blood orange soda. Tart, slightly bitter, Sicilian blood orange profile. Not a Fanta.
- Available in 330ml glass bottles and cans.

**Limestone Springs Root Beer**
- Traditional American root beer with sassafras, vanilla, wintergreen. Kentucky heritage recipe.
- Available in 330ml glass bottles and cans.
- The nostalgia play — compound barbecues, Kentucky summers, Americana.

**Limestone Springs Cream Soda**
- Vanilla cream soda. Rich, smooth. Made with real vanilla bean.
- Available in 330ml glass bottles and cans.

**Limestone Springs Bitter Lemon**
- Classic bitter lemon. Quinine + lemon. Strong in European markets.
- Available in 200ml glass bottles.

**Limestone Springs Soda Water / Club Soda**
- The purest expression of the limestone water — just carbonation. Bar staple.
- Available in 200ml glass bottles (bar standard), 750ml glass bottles.

**Limestone Springs Tonic Syrup**
- Concentrated tonic syrup for home bartenders and professional bar programmes. Add to sparkling water for custom-strength tonic. Premium product, small batch.
- Available in 500ml bottles. Retail: $14-18.

## DISTRIBUTION

### On-Trade (Bars, Restaurants, Hotels)

The primary revenue channel (~55% of revenue). Limestone Springs positions itself as the premium mixer and water choice for bars and restaurants that care about quality.

**Hatfield properties:** All Hatfield Bar & Grill locations (16), The Stave (2), Hatfield Inns (8), Still House — Limestone Springs is the exclusive water, tonic, and mixer brand. This is a captive channel representing ~8% of total Limestone Springs revenue.

**Independent bars and restaurants:** The growth engine. Limestone Springs is stocked in:
- Premium cocktail bars globally (the ginger beer and tonic water are bartender favourites)
- High-end restaurants (the sparkling water is a credible Pellegrino/Perrier alternative with a better story)
- Hotel bars and room service (Hatfield Inns + independent premium hotels)
- Bourbon bars specifically — the "same water as the bourbon" angle is genuine and powerful

**Airlines:** Premium cabin contracts with two major carriers (undisclosed). Tonic water and sparkling water served in business and first class. High-margin, high-visibility channel.

### Off-Trade (Retail)

~30% of revenue. Growing faster than on-trade.

**US:** National distribution through Whole Foods, specialty food retailers, Total Wine & More (alongside Hatfield bourbon). Growing presence in mainstream grocery (Kroger, Publix in select markets). Amazon and DTC website.

**UK:** Supermarket placement growing (Waitrose, Sainsbury's premium aisle). Strong in independent food retailers and delicatessens. Online through Ocado.

**EU:** Germany, France, Netherlands, Scandinavia. Specialty retail and premium grocery.

**Japan:** Distributed through premium import channels. Strong in Tokyo cocktail bars and high-end retail.

**Australia:** Established. Dan Murphy's, independent retailers.

**Middle East:** Dubai, Abu Dhabi. Premium hotel and restaurant placement. Non-alcoholic positioning is an advantage.

### E-Commerce & DTC

~15% of revenue. Growing.
- Direct website: subscription model for regular delivery (mixer packs, soda variety cases)
- Amazon: US, UK, EU marketplaces
- Gift sets: Hatfield bourbon + Limestone Springs mixer pairings. Strong holiday season.

## THE PRODUCTION FACILITY

**Location:** Frankfort, Kentucky. Purpose-built bottling and production facility on company-owned land adjacent to the limestone spring source.

**Facility:**
- Modern bottling lines: glass bottle line (for on-trade 200ml/330ml/750ml) + PET line (for retail) + canning line (added 2019 for soda range)
- Blending and flavouring room: where tonic, ginger beer, and sodas are formulated and mixed
- Quality control laboratory: every batch tested for mineral content, carbonation levels, flavour consistency
- Cold storage and warehouse: temperature-controlled for ingredient freshness
- Shipping/logistics: direct distribution to US regional hubs; consolidated shipping for international

**Source:** Natural limestone spring. Permitted and regularly tested. Production capacity well above current bottling demand — the spring can support 3-4x current production volume without stress.

**Employees:** ~85 (plant manager, production supervisors, bottling line operators, QC team, logistics/warehouse, sales team, marketing, admin). The sales and marketing team has grown significantly since the global expansion — 15 of the 85 are in sales and business development across US, UK, and international markets.

## THE BOURBON CONNECTION

The marketing proposition is authentic, not manufactured: *"Made with the same limestone-filtered water that has made Kentucky bourbon for 250 years."*

This isn't a tagline bolted onto a generic product. The geological reality — Kentucky's limestone karst system naturally filtering groundwater, adding minerals, creating the water profile that bourbon distillers have relied on since the 18th century — is the foundation of both the distillery and the beverage company. Limestone Springs doesn't just reference bourbon; it shares the same source.

**The bartender conversation:** When a bartender pours Hatfield Single Barrel and reaches for a Limestone Springs tonic to make a bourbon-tonic, the water in both products came from the same geological formation. That's a story that sells itself. It's also true.

## NARRATOR RULE — DO NOT EXPLAIN THE BRAND IN SCENE

**Limestone Springs is a brand. Not a concept. Not a product to be introduced.**

When Limestone Springs appears in a scene, treat it exactly the way you'd treat Fever-Tree, Pellegrino, or Perrier. Nobody explains what those are when a character orders one. Don't do it with Limestone Springs either.

**Wrong:**
> Alex reached for the Limestone Springs — a Kentucky-sourced sparkling water his family's company made, built on the same limestone geology as the bourbon.

**Right:**
> Alex poured a Limestone Springs over ice and handed it across.

The geological story, the brand proposition, the global distribution — none of that belongs in scene narration. It lives in this file. In scene, it's just what's in the fridge at the compound, what's on the table at Hatfield Lexington, what gets poured when someone wants something non-alcoholic. A thing Alex has been drinking his whole life without thinking about it.
