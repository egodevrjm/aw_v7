## THE OPENING PRINCIPLE

**Hatfield Licensing is the brand extension engine — the division that takes everything Hatfield has built in bourbon, food, and hospitality and scales it through licensing and franchising.**

Two operations under one roof:

1. **Hatfield Provisions** — condiments, sauces, and specialty food products sold through retail. Asset-light: recipes and brand licensed to co-manufacturers. The Hatfield name in your pantry.

2. **Hatfield Franchise Operations** — the franchise arm for Hatfield Bar & Grill and Hatfield Inns. 20-40 franchised locations in early growth, with aggressive expansion planned. The machine that turns a 16-restaurant, 8-hotel company into a global hospitality brand.

Both operations are fundamentally the same business: licensing the Hatfield brand, standards, and playbook to partners who execute locally. Provisions licenses recipes to co-packers. Franchise Operations licenses the restaurant and hotel concept to franchisees. Different products, same model: brand control + quality standards + partner execution.

# PART TWO: HATFIELD FRANCHISE OPERATIONS

## The Franchise Model

Hatfield Franchise Operations is the growth engine that takes the company-owned Bar & Grill and Inns model and licenses it to franchisees worldwide. Launched in 2023, the programme is in early growth — 20-40 franchised locations open or in build-out, with an aggressive expansion pipeline.

**Why franchise:**
- Company-owned expansion is capital-intensive ($3-5M per restaurant, $15-25M per hotel). Franchising lets the brand grow faster with partner capital.
- The brand, operational playbook, training programmes, and supply chain are mature enough to franchise without quality dilution.
- Franchise revenue compounds: joining fees + ongoing royalties + mandatory Hatfield product supply (bourbon, mixers, provisions).
- The model is proven: 16 company-owned Bar & Grills and 8 company-owned Inns demonstrate concept viability across diverse markets.

## FRANCHISE SUPPORT OPERATIONS

The franchise team (~20 people, Louisville-based) provides:

### Pre-Opening

| Function | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Site selection** | Franchise team evaluates proposed locations against brand criteria (neighbourhood profile, foot traffic, competitive landscape, demographics). Final approval required. |
| **Design review** | Each franchise location submits design plans. Hatfield design team reviews for brand compliance while allowing local character. |
| **Training** | 8-week programme (B&G) or 6-week programme (Inns). Every franchisee's GM and key staff trained at Louisville HQ and company-owned properties. |
| **Supply chain setup** | Bourbon supply contracts, Limestone Springs distribution, Provisions product ordering — all set up before opening. |
| **Opening support** | Hatfield team on-site for first 2 weeks of operation. Troubleshooting, quality standards, staff coaching. |

### Ongoing

| Function | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Quality audits** | Quarterly unannounced visits by franchise operations team. Bourbon programme, food quality, service standards, design maintenance scored. |
| **Bourbon education** | Annual refresher training for franchise bar staff. New product introductions (Reserve allocations, seasonal releases). |
| **Marketing** | National and regional campaigns funded by marketing fund contributions. Social media templates, seasonal promotion calendars, event playbooks. |
| **Supply chain** | Ongoing bourbon, mixer, and provisions supply at wholesale pricing. Distribution logistics managed centrally. |
| **Performance benchmarking** | Monthly financial reporting. Franchise locations benchmarked against each other and against company-owned properties. Underperformers get support; persistent underperformers face franchise review. |

### The Quality Line

David Buckner is clear: the franchise programme exists to grow the brand, not to dilute it. Franchise agreements include quality termination clauses — if a franchised location consistently fails quality audits, the franchise can be revoked. This has not yet happened (the programme is young), but the clause exists and franchisees are informed during training.

The bourbon programme is non-negotiable. A franchised Hatfield Bar & Grill must stock the full Hatfield range, maintain trained bar staff, and deliver the same bourbon experience as a company-owned location. If the bourbon programme slips, the brand slips.

## WHAT THIS MEANS FOR HATFIELD GROUP

The Licensing division transforms Hatfield from a company that operates ~30 company-owned properties into a brand that has **100+ locations worldwide** within a few years — without proportional capital investment. The company-owned locations remain the quality benchmark and brand standard-setters. The franchise locations are the scale play.

**The virtuous cycle:**
- More franchise locations → more bourbon sold (mandatory supply) → more distillery revenue
- More franchise locations → more Limestone Springs mixer sales → more beverage revenue
- More franchise locations → more Provisions condiment placements → more retail awareness
- More brand awareness → stronger franchise applications → better franchisees → better quality → more brand value

David Buckner's pitch to the Hatfield board: Licensing is the division that makes every other division more valuable.

## NARRATOR RULE — FRANCHISE LOCATIONS ARE JUST HATFIELD

**When a scene is set at a franchised Hatfield location, it's still just Hatfield. Don't flag the ownership structure.**

Alex doesn't walk into a franchised Bar & Grill and think "this is a franchise unit, not company-owned." It's Hatfield. He sits down. If the quality is right, he wouldn't know the difference and wouldn't care.

**Wrong:**
> The Hatfield in Dublin was a franchise location — independently owned under licence from the group, maintaining brand standards through quarterly audits.

**Right:**
> There was a Hatfield in Dublin. Alex hadn't been to this one before.

The franchise mechanics belong in this file. In scene, it's just another location of a place he knows.
