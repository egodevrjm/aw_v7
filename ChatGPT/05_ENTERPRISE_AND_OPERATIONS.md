### Greystone Services — What They Provide

- **2 guards on-site 24/7.** Armed. Rotating shifts. Greystone employees.

#### 3. Advance Work

# PART TWO: HATFIELD FRANCHISE OPERATIONS

## The Franchise Model

Hatfield Franchise Operations is the growth engine that takes the company-owned Bar & Grill and Inns model and licenses it to franchisees worldwide. Launched in 2023, the programme is in early growth — 20-40 franchised locations open or in build-out, with an aggressive expansion pipeline.

**Why franchise:**
- Company-owned expansion is capital-intensive ($3-5M per restaurant, $15-25M per hotel). Franchising lets the brand grow faster with partner capital.
- The brand, operational playbook, training programmes, and supply chain are mature enough to franchise without quality dilution.
- Franchise revenue compounds: joining fees + ongoing royalties + mandatory Hatfield product supply (bourbon, mixers, provisions).
- The model is proven: 16 company-owned Bar & Grills and 8 company-owned Inns demonstrate concept viability across diverse markets.

**Limestone Springs is a premium soda, mixer, and sparkling water company built on the same Kentucky limestone geology that makes bourbon possible — and it has gone global.**

## THE STORY

**2002:** Hatfield Group started bottling Kentucky limestone spring water as a complementary product — something to serve alongside bourbon at the distillery and the restaurants. Simple operation: source, bottle, distribute locally.

**2005-2014:** The water gained a regional following. Kentucky, Tennessee, Ohio, Indiana — restaurants and bars that took their water seriously started stocking Limestone Springs. The mineral profile (naturally filtered through 300M-year-old limestone, adding calcium and magnesium, removing iron) gave the water a distinctive clean, slightly sweet character. Bourbon bars particularly liked it: the same water that makes their bourbon, served at the table.

**2015 — The pivot:** David Buckner saw the opportunity. The premium mixer market was exploding globally — Fever-Tree had proven that people would pay for quality tonic, quality ginger beer, quality mixers. The cocktail renaissance meant bartenders cared about every ingredient, including what they mixed with. Limestone Springs had the source water, the brand, and the Hatfield connection. Buckner hired a beverage director, invested in production capability, and expanded from water into a full mixer and craft soda range.

**2018-present — Global expansion:** The mixer range launched into the UK first (natural synergy with The Stave London and the growing bourbon cocktail scene). Then EU, Japan, Australia. Airline contracts followed (premium cabins stock Limestone Springs tonic and sparkling). Retail distribution expanded globally. The brand now generates more revenue from mixers and sodas than from water.

- **The first two cards were private.** When writing the Collins rollout, the first public-adjacent moment is AL.X letterhead post-June-5, not the business cards or the personal stationery.
