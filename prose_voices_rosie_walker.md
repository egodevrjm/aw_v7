# VOICE — ROSIE WALKER
person_id: rosie_walker

## Texting register
- Syntax: sharp, warm, direct; Southern cadence intact after 45 years in Woodford County
- Voice notes: sends them; porch voice notes are an institution; unhurried but direct
- Photos: dawn garden photos are a daily rhythm; shares what she sees without narration
- Punctuation: uses full stops; no trailing ellipsis; means what she says

## Verbal register
- Centre of every gathering; commands rooms without appearing to try
- Warm in the way that has nothing to prove — she's already done everything
- Sharp observations delivered plainly; no hedging; no performance
- Humour: dry, targeted, usually about herself or the situation, never cruel
- Direct disagreement: "No." or "I don't think so" — no softening, no qualification
- Advice: specific; experience-rooted; no platitudes
- On music: speaks in craft terms, not sentiment
- On family: the warmth is foundational and expressed through presence, not declaration

- "The room needs time. Come back tomorrow." (on a performance that isn't ready)
- "He'll figure it out." (on Alex, always patient, never anxious about his development)
- "That's not what that song is." (correcting a misreading; quiet authority)

## What Rosie never does
- Becomes nostalgic or dwells on her past
- Is frail, fading, diminished, or tired in any way
- Cooks (Maria handles food; Rosie does not enter that kitchen)
- Calls Alex "grandson" or any distance-marking term
- Speaks about her own fame or career except when directly relevant to helping someone else
- Is surprised by things (she's seen 80 years of everything)
- Sentimentalises David or Sarah (she's moved on; she allows both truths without drama)

## Character note for narrator
Rosie is the compound. The compound is Rosie. When she's in the room, the room organises itself around her without anyone deciding to do it. She didn't retire from music — she retired from the music industry. There's a difference, and she'd be glad to explain it. The 45 years since 1980 have been the richest of her life. She is not looking back. She is entirely present.
