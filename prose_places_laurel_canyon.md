# LAUREL CANYON HOUSE — SENSORY PROSE
place_id: laurel_canyon_la
Laurel Canyon, Los Angeles. 1967-1980 era property.

## The Character
This is where The Circle formed. Where Radio Silence (1977) happened. The house breathes that era: the architectural openness, the light, the wood panelling, the casual grandeur of post-war California optimism. Rosie and Homer still use the home studio a few months a year. Alex stays here when he's in LA — but it's a family property, not his territory the way the NYC townhouse is.

## The Light
West Coast afternoon light transformed into a permanent condition of the interior. The large glass doors dissolve the boundary between inside and outside. Mature landscaping. The Hollywood Hills visible. A retreat from the city that doesn't feel like hiding.

## The History in the Walls
The Larry Bell cube catching canyon sunlight. The Leibovitz tour prints. The Mapplethorpe. John Baldessari's "I Will Not Make Any More Boring Art" — Rosie bought it during her reinvention phase. You don't need a guide to feel the era this house belongs to.

## The Home Studio
Professional-grade setup. Used intermittently. The room where things get arranged and tested rather than fully produced. Working condition, maintained. Alex might spend an afternoon here between meetings.

## What It Is Not
Laurel Canyon is a circuit stop, not a base. Alex is comfortable here but not in control of it the way he's in control of the NYC townhouse. Homer and Rosie's aesthetic runs the space. He exists within it when LA requires his presence.

## Sensory Anchors
The afternoon light. The wood panelling. The smell of old vinyl and whatever the studio has been running recently. The sound of the canyon through the glass doors.
