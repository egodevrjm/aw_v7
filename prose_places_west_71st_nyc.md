# WEST 71ST STREET — SENSORY PROSE
place_id: west_71st_nyc
Upper West Side, New York City. 6-story brownstone.

## The Character
This is Alex's property in the fullest sense. Unlike the compound (family home, decades of history) or Kensington (Homer's meticulous space), the NYC townhouse is entirely his making. Every aesthetic choice, every piece of art, every room's function — built between 18 and 21. When he moves through it he moves with full ownership.

## Entering
The street noise filtered through Homer's soundproofing — you know it's there but it's muffled, distant. The Richard Prince in the entryway (dry, cynical humour, the first thing you see). The proportions of a serious house but not a formal one.

## The Salon (Parlor Level)
Double-height ceilings. The 1970 Steinway Model B — different character from the compound D: smaller, brighter, more intimate. The Donald Judd "stack" (1968) cantilevered floor to ceiling in warm amber Plexiglas and cool stainless steel. Everything else on this floor exists in relationship to the Judd. The Basquiat facing the Steinway.

## The Library (Second Floor)
Where Alex actually lives during the day. Floor-to-ceiling bookshelves. Reading chair. The turntable with the vinyl collection he built in three Juilliard years. Warm and book-dense and worked-in, not a showpiece.

## The Basement Pool
33-foot lap pool. Barrel-vaulted ceiling. Mosaic tile. Steam room adjoining. The architecture of the water is extraordinary for a building in Manhattan.

## The Roof Terrace
The views through the treeline toward Central Park. Summer evenings up here are why Alex loves this house.

## Sensory Anchors
The Steinway in the salon. The reading light in the library. The walk to Lincoln Center he did a thousand times. The doorman who knows his name. Coffee from the place on the corner. The rear garden impossibly green and quiet for Manhattan.
