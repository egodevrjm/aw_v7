# ALEX'S COCKTAIL DOCUMENT
doc_id: alex_cocktails
canonical_reference: C34 (archive/CORRECTIONS.md)

> **What this is:** Alex keeps a live Google Doc of cocktails — drinks he makes, drinks he orders, drinks he is working on, recipes he has picked up from specific bars. He has been maintaining it since he was seventeen. The doc lives in his Google account, shared with no one. The Rickhouse Julep is one item in this document among many. Do not default to the Rickhouse Julep as his signature drink. He has a range.

> **Canonical reason this file exists:** Session drift kept reducing Alex's cocktail identity to a single drink. The reality is a wide repertoire with a specific shape. When a scene calls for Alex making or ordering a cocktail, pull from this list appropriately for the context.

---

## SHAPE OF THE DOCUMENT

Alex's doc is organised by category, not alphabetically. He thinks about cocktails the way he thinks about music — genre, era, mood. Each drink has a short note about when it fits: season, setting, company.

The document is in his handwriting voice, which is to say: short, deadpan, occasionally funny, never precious. He would be quietly mortified if someone called this a "craft cocktail journal." He does not use that phrase.

## 1. BOURBON — THE KENTUCKY BASELINE

These are the drinks that come from the culture he grew up in. Hatfield is in this section. His great-grandfather Homer made bourbon; he grew up smelling rickhouses.

- **Rickhouse Julep** — Hatfield 12-year, mint from the Phoenix Hollow kitchen garden, Demerara syrup made with Hatfield's first-run molasses, shaved ice. His note: *"The one Tommy asks for. Maria's mint only. Do not substitute."*
- **The Phoenix** — Hatfield Reserve, Angostura, orange bitters, luxardo cherry, orange peel expressed. Old Fashioned with a specific hand. His note: *"What Homer drinks. Do not over-build."*
- **Backroad Manhattan** — Hatfield 15-year, Antica Formula, two dashes Angostura, black walnut bitters. His note: *"Nashville session guys. A little more sweet than the city build."*
- **Derby Week** — bourbon, mint, lemon, Hatfield honey syrup, sparkling rosé float. His note: *"Made up for Rosie's Derby brunch 2023. She drank three."*
- **The Hollow Julep** — Hatfield 21-year, single ice spear, no mint, no sweet, one bitters dash. *"For when the drink is the drink."*
- **Paper Plane** — bourbon, Aperol, Amaro Nonino, lemon. A Sam Ross original; Alex has the spec memorised. His note: *"Sam's drink. Do not mess with it."*

## 2. CLASSICS — KNOW THEM COLD

The canon. He can make all of these without thinking.

- **Martini** — gin-forward, London dry, dry vermouth (Dolin), lemon twist. *"Stirred. Always."*
- **Negroni** — equal parts, Campari, Carpano Antica, gin. Orange peel. *"Nothing to do. The drink already works."*
- **Boulevardier** — equal parts, bourbon in for gin, same build. *"Winter version of the Negroni."*
- **Old Fashioned** — bourbon or rye, Demerara, Angostura, orange. *"Build in the glass."*
- **Manhattan** — rye preferred, Antica, Angostura, cherry. *"Rye unless specified."*
- **Sazerac** — rye, Peychaud's, absinthe rinse, lemon oil. *"Chilled glass. Don't skip the rinse."*
- **Daiquiri** — rum, lime, simple. *"Hemingway was right about one thing."*
- **Margarita** — tequila blanco, lime, Cointreau. Salt rim optional (he skips). *"Don't add orange juice. Ever."*
- **Whiskey Sour** — bourbon, lemon, simple, egg white. *"The egg is not optional."*
- **Corpse Reviver №2** — gin, Cointreau, Lillet Blanc, lemon, absinthe rinse. *"Brunch drink. Stealth weapon."*

## 3. EUROPEAN — SUMMERS IN SARDINIA AND ITALY

The drinks he developed a palate for on the Costa Smeralda and in Milan. Aperitivo-forward, bitter-forward, not sweet.

- **Americano** — Campari, sweet vermouth, soda, orange. *"First drink of the night. Always."*
- **Spritz** — Aperol or Cynar or Select, prosecco, soda, orange. *"Never syrupy. If it's syrupy, pour it out."*
- **Garibaldi** — Campari, fresh orange juice, high foam. *"Dante's build. The blender is the trick."*
- **Bicicletta** — Campari, white wine, soda, lemon. *"Afternoon. Lido."*
- **White Negroni** — gin, Suze, Lillet Blanc, grapefruit peel. *"Summer Negroni."*
- **Limoncello Spritz** — limoncello, prosecco, soda, lemon. *"Rosie's mother's recipe. Amalfi only."*
- **Bellini** — white peach purée, prosecco. *"Harry's Bar, Venice. Do not modernise."*

## 4. GIN — LONDON

Six years at Westminster, then the RAM years, then Kensington. He has a London gin palate.

- **Gimlet** — gin, lime cordial (fresh made), nothing else. *"Rose's lime if the cordial isn't done."*
- **Tom Collins** — Plymouth gin, lemon, simple, soda. *"Summer. Freddie's kitchen counter."*
- **Aviation** — gin, maraschino, crème de violette, lemon. *"Violet is a trap. Half measure max."*
- **Bee's Knees** — gin, lemon, honey syrup. *"Honey from the Kensington garden."*
- **French 75** — gin, lemon, sugar, Champagne. *"Not sparkling wine. Champagne."*
- **Vesper** — gin, vodka, Lillet Blanc. *"Bond was wrong about most things."*

## 5. LOW-ABV / AFTERNOON

He drinks moderately, especially during the day. These are the drinks that let him stay in a social setting without being drunk.

- **Sbagliato** — Campari, sweet vermouth, prosecco instead of gin. *"The mistake drink. Not a mistake."*
- **Hugo Spritz** — elderflower, prosecco, soda, mint, lime. *"Alpine summer. Dolomites."*
- **Sherry Cobbler** — amontillado, orange, lemon, sugar, crushed ice, mint. *"Morning cocktail if morning cocktails were a thing."*
- **Vermouth on the Rocks** — Carpano Antica, orange peel, cube ice. *"Before dinner. One."*

## 6. HOSTING — WHEN PEOPLE COME OVER

The drinks he makes when friends are over at West 71st or Kensington. Things that scale.

- **Batched Negroni** — 1:1:1 pre-batched, refrigerated, served over ice with fresh peel. *"For parties. Same drink, no bottleneck."*
- **Pitcher Paloma** — tequila blanco, fresh grapefruit, lime, salt rim, sparkling. *"Summer cookouts."*
- **Pitcher Americano** — batched, served tall. *"Sardinia baseline."*
- **Champagne Punch (Rosie's recipe)** — Champagne, cognac, lemon, sugar, cucumber, mint. *"Rosie's dinner parties for forty years."*

## 7. PROJECTS — CURRENTLY WORKING ON

The section that is always half-done. Drinks he is still refining.

- **The Fletching** — trying to build a drink around the Fletching Park landscape. Currently: gin, elderflower, Earl Grey syrup, lemon, a drop of something he hasn't named. Unfinished.
- **Coat-Check Stinger** — crème de menthe drink, rebuilt. The version he had at Zero Bond last winter was almost right. Closer each time. *"Three more tries."*
- **A Negroni with something fortified from the Hatfield cave programme** — prototype, nameless.
- **An Armagnac-based old fashioned** — after a Paris trip in 2024. The armagnac is right; the bitters are wrong.

## 8. WHAT HE ORDERS WHEN OUT (not a recipe section)

- **At a new bar with a good bartender:** whatever the bartender wants to make him. He asks. He drinks it.
- **At a hotel bar:** Martini. Every time.
- **At Dante:** Garibaldi.
- **At The Stave NYC bar:** asks the bar director what's new. Defers.
- **At a Kentucky place:** Old Fashioned.
- **On a plane:** nothing. He doesn't drink on flights.
- **Before a performance:** nothing until after.

## NARRATOR NOTES

- **Do not default to Rickhouse Julep.** It is his most-recognised drink but it is one of forty-plus in this doc. Match the drink to the scene. Summer Sardinia = Americano or Negroni Sbagliato. NYC dinner = probably a Negroni or a Martini. Compound summer evening = Julep absolutely fits. London night-in = Gin. Afternoon work-adjacent = Sbagliato or Americano.
- **The doc is never visible on-screen.** No character ever asks to see it. It exists as his thinking, not as a public document.
- **He is modest about it.** If someone compliments a drink, he says "thanks." If pressed, he says "Dante's build" or "Sam's drink" or "old family thing." Rarely takes credit.
- **Range, not virtuosity.** He is a good home bartender, not a professional. His builds are correct and clean. He does not do flair. He does not do dry ice or edible flowers or smoke under cloches. The drinks are classically built and well executed.
