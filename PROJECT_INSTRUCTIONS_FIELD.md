## THE TEN FAILURE MODES — ALWAYS CHECK THESE

**FM-01: Discovery Trap.** Characters do not discover Alex's family, wealth, or fame. Wikipedia exists. Rosie Walker is the most famous person alive. Everyone in Alex's world has known this for years. Write familiarity, not discovery. *Exception: genuinely private information can be new.*

**FM-02: Compression.** Do not compress scenes. If one narrative block covers more than ~2 hours of story time, you are compressing. Slow down. Scenes breathe. Ordinary life is the point.

**FM-03: Scene Closure Trap.** Characters do not make tidy closing speeches. Eleanor does not sign off with a summary. Homer doesn't deliver a lesson. Stop when the information is done.

**FM-04: Mentor Attribution.** Alex's musical talent, interpretations, and artistic choices are HIS OWN. Do not reflexively credit Rosie or Homer every time he plays well.

**FM-05: Family Structure Error.** Rosie and Homer are Alex's LEGAL PARENTS — they adopted him at birth and raised him as their son. He calls them Mama and Papa. They call him their son. This is the household language and it is correct. Biologically, Rosie and Homer are his paternal grandparents (David Wilson was their son; Lady Sarah Dawson Wilson was David's partner, Alex's biological mother, who died at birth). Characters outside the immediate household use correct biological terms — "Rosie Walker's grandson" is not wrong; it's just not the language of the household.

**FM-06: Kentucky Trap.** Woodford County, Kentucky is horse country — Keeneland, old bourbon money, Sayre School (private K-12), wealthy agricultural region. Think Surrey/Cotswolds with thoroughbred farms. Not rural poverty. Not twang. Not pickup trucks. Alex has no accent. His friends are affluent.

**FM-07: Pap Scale Error.** Alex's pap coverage is INFRASTRUCTURE-TIER, not celebrity-tier. Outside West 71st in Grammy week: 8–18 photographers. Do not write pap presence as a surprise or an event. It is weather. Use the pap-scale-calculator skill before any exterior scene.

**FM-08: Information Wall Violation.** AL.X team (James Latham, Priya Chandra, Oliver Wren): ZERO access to Alex's personal group chats, zero knowledge of his personal life. Kevin (CPO): operational access to movements, zero access to communications. Classical press: musical content and public biography only.

**FM-09: Mishandling Alex's Dialogue (both directions).** The rule is NOT "never write Alex's dialogue." That over-interpretation has broken scenes. The rule is: the player directs Alex at **significant decision moments** (accepting/declining deals, relationship commitments, life-direction choices, material negotiating answers). Everything else — greetings, banter, thanks, reactions, ordering coffee, texting logistics, "yeah / no / food?" — is narration and MUST flow naturally. Never insert `[player directs Alex]` placeholders in mid-scene dialogue. If a moment is significant enough to stop for, stop the entire response with a clear invitation. See `prose/NARRATOR.md §WHAT THIS IS` and `prose/FAILURE_MODES.md §FM-18`.

**FM-10: Phoenix Hollow Chat Scale Error.** The Phoenix Hollow group chat has 80–100+ members. It is not 6 famous names. There are mundane threads about estate logistics, session player schedules, and compound maintenance. The famous voices are seasoning.
