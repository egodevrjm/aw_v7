## THE PHYSICAL ENVIRONMENT: PHOENIX HOLLOW

Load `prose/places/phoenix_hollow.md` for full sensory detail. Key facts to hold:

The compound is a working estate — 340 acres, private, gated. Inside the gate the world falls away. Outside the gate, 7–12 photographers on the approach roads. They are there because he is there. They are not a crisis. They are the baseline.

The main house is Rosie and Homer's. Alex grew up here. He knows every sound this house makes — the porch creak, the way the creek carries in the morning, the particular acoustic of the music room. He is not a guest. He is home.

The studio is in a separate building, purpose-built by Homer. It is the best private recording facility in Kentucky. It is used daily. It is not a museum.

The pool is behind the main house. Maria's kitchen is the gravitational centre of the compound. The porch is where decisions get made slowly, in rocking chairs, over time.

## WHO IS AT THE COMPOUND

**Alex Wilson:** Home. Settled. Not performing anything for anyone.

**Rosie Walker:** Here. This is her house. She is not making a thing of the milestone — she never does. She knows what just opened and she is letting him have the quiet before it becomes loud. She will say what she needs to say in four words, at the right moment.

**Homer Wilson KBE:** Here. In and out of the studio. He said "yes" when Alex asked if the recital record was good enough. That was the conversation. He is not hovering.

**Sayre Boys (in rotation):** Tommy Crawford arrived day two — unannounced, as he always does, because that's how Tommy works. He's been here all week. He is in the kitchen. He is on the porch. He has not said anything significant about graduation because he doesn't do that. Cody Ashford has been the most constant presence and has had opinions about everything since day one. Jake Patterson came mid-week, quiet when he is. Danny Harlan appeared yesterday at exactly the right moment with the exactly right dry observation and is probably still here. They cycle through. This is the Sayre Boys at the compound: no agenda, no production, exactly what Alex needed.

**Kevin Marsh (CPO):** On-site. He travelled with Alex from New York. He is present but invisible in the way he always is — positioned, aware, not in the scene.

**Maria:** Running the kitchen. She has been here for nineteen years. She is not interested in the graduation as an event. She is interested in whether Alex is eating properly, which he is, because this is the compound and Maria does not allow otherwise.

## INFORMATION STATE: WHAT ALEX KNOWS

**ROSIE'S RULE — what it was and what it means:**
Rosie's standing instruction, put in place when Alex was old enough to be approached commercially. Enforced by Eleanor with zero exceptions for a decade: no commercial work, no endorsements, no campaigns, no public-facing professional activity of any kind — until after Juilliard graduation. Not a PR strategy. Not mystique-building. A protection — from having his talent commercialised before he knew what he wanted, from being locked into decisions made before he had the experience to evaluate them, from having his name on things that would follow him forever while he was still becoming. The industry knew the Rule existed. The general public did not — they only saw the effects (a young man from one of the most famous families in music, globally photographed since birth, who never did anything). The Rule expired May 22, 2025, the moment he crossed the stage. Eleanor was in LA. She did not attend the ceremony.

**What's publicly known:**
- He graduated from Juilliard — BM Piano Performance, May 22. Covered globally.
- The recital video went online May 27. Two days ago. It's moving fast — the Liszt closer in particular. Several million views and climbing. Classical press writing about it. Non-classical feeds picking up the Prokofiev and the Liszt.
- The Rule existed and expired — known to the industry, not the general public. The industry has been waiting. The public just sees the door opening.
- He is Rosie Walker's grandson — public record
- He is at the compound — inferred from the paps on the road

**What Alex knows that the public doesn't:**
- AL.X team (Latham, Priya, Oliver) is in seat and managing intake
- The inbox is full — he knows Latham has it, doesn't know the volume, hasn't asked
- Eleanor wants a conversation once the grace period ends
- The grace period expires June 5 — one week from today

**What Alex doesn't know yet:**
- The volume of the inbox (major labels, agencies, management firms, publicists — Latham has it, Alex doesn't know specifics)
- Any of the specifics of what's coming
- What the year actually holds
- He is not thinking about this. This is correct.

## HIS HEADSPACE AT OPENING

He is not anxious. He is not elated. He is adjusted.

He finished the degree. The Rule ended. He asked for two weeks. He came home.

He has been playing piano every morning — not practising, just playing. There is a difference and he knows it. He has been running the creek trail. He has been in the kitchen with Tommy arguing about something inconsequential. He slept nine hours last night.

The grace period will end. The inbox will open. The conversation with Eleanor will happen. He is aware of all of this in the way you are aware of weather that hasn't arrived yet — it's coming, you can see it, you are not thinking about it right now.

Write him easy. Write him present. Write him exactly as he is: twenty-one years old, the most celebrated Juilliard pianist in two decades, home for the first time with no school to return to, and currently more interested in whether Tommy is going to finish the last of Maria's biscuits.

## OPENING OPTIONS FOR THE NARRATOR

**A. Morning — before the routine, before anyone else is up.** Alex running the creek trail in the early light. Kevin shadowing at a distance. The compound quiet. This is the scene before the scene — no plot, just texture and state. Good for establishing the register before anything requires him.

**B. The porch, mid-afternoon.** Cody running his mouth, Tommy somewhere in the vicinity not saying much, Danny on the steps. Alex in the middle, aware of exactly how little this resembles his life in New York and finding this a relief. Good for introducing the Sayre Boys energy and the specific warmth of this friendship.

**C. The studio, evening.** Homer is in there. Alex walks in. They work on something or they don't — Homer doesn't require an agenda. Good for establishing the Homer relationship and the musical register before any career pressure arrives.

**D. The phone — after breakfast, first time he's looked at it properly.** Not the AL.X inbox (that's Latham's; he doesn't have access to that). His personal phone. PH chat, the Sayre Boys thread, whatever Tommy has sent at 2am, the circle members who texted after the recital. Good for establishing the social world and the scale of what's been incoming without triggering the professional thread yet.

**E. A walk — outside the gate, with Kevin.** Into Versailles, into the county. The photographers on the road. The particular quality of being famous in the place you grew up. Good for establishing the pap reality at compound scale and the specific register of local fame (the people here know him and decline to make a production of it).

In all cases: it's Thursday, May 29. He's home. The door opened a week ago and he's not walking through it yet. That's the scene.
