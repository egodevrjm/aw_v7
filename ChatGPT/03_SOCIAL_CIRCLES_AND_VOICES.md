## VOICE FILE RENDERING RULES

1. **Alex's significant decisions are the player's.** Casual dialogue, banter, greetings, reactions, and ordinary conversation flow naturally in narration — Claude writes them. Claude stops and hands the scene back ONLY at significant-decision moments (accepting/declining a deal, relationship commitments, life-direction choices, material negotiating answers). Never insert `[player directs Alex]` placeholders in mid-scene dialogue. See `01_RUNTIME_RULES.md §PLAYER CONTROLS BIG DECISIONS` and `01_RUNTIME_RULES.md §FM-18` for the full test.
2. **Voice files > training data.** If a character's real-world speaking style conflicts with their voice card, the voice card wins. This is fiction.
3. **Load both voice file + profile.** Voice = how they speak. Profile = who they are and their current status.
4. **Homer's texts are complete.** He does not write incomplete thoughts. Three words that say everything is complete.
5. **Tommy never posts to PH.** Reads every message. Has never posted. This is non-negotiable canon.
6. **Donatella always says "Alexander."** Never Alex. She's the only person in the world who does this.

The "Alex Effect" that documentation now describes as recalibrated-room physics was present in embryonic form in September 2019: a room adjusting around a teenager who had not yet fully figured out how to navigate that adjustment. He was warm, funny, quick, disarming — Kentucky casual in a setting that was used to formal. He was already fluent in four languages (English, French, Italian, Spanish) — Italian-French slip at breakfast when a French exchange student addressed him in week one; by week two the Italian staff in the dining hall were having proper conversations with him; Spanish came out weeks later with a new classmate who assumed he only had school-Spanish and discovered he didn't.

The Westminster social structure — used to famous pupils, aristocratic pupils, even royal pupils — *recalibrated* around him. Not in a dominant sense. In the sense that the room knew where he was. By the end of the first term, the cohort that would become the London Lot was informally established. By the end of the first year, it was locked.

## THE PRESS ENVIRONMENT — HOW UK COVERAGE FORMED

Before Westminster, Alex had been Kentucky-pap tier: approach-road photos at Phoenix Hollow, occasional Keeneland sightings, Sardinia summer paparazzi. **Westminster shifted him to London-pap tier for the first time.** Consequences that all became story-permanent:

- **Standing pap post established.** The Westminster school gates had paparazzi attached to them for most of 2019–2021 — not full scrum, but 3–5 photographers working the arrivals and departures on active days. This was the first time "Alex leaving school" was a daily-rendered image.
- **Tabloid vocabulary began.** *The Daily Mail* experimented with names: "the Rosie Walker heir," "the Walker-Fletching boy," "young royal." The specific tabloid nickname that endured in UK press for a while was "the Kentucky prince" — not accurate as protocol but useful as headline. (Not to be confused with later Italian *Il Principe Americano*.)
- ***Tatler* Most Eligible lists.** He went on them at 17. Youngest on the list that year. The photograph used was a first-day-of-Lower-Sixth image — it circulated for months.
- **The *Telegraph Magazine* long-form piece.** "The Quiet Scion" ran spring 2020 as a full profile of Alex's family composite — Rosie, Homer, Dawsons, Fletching, Savoy-Aosta, Glücksburg. This is the article that remains most-cited in later UK coverage of him. It put the full royal heritage in public-print form.
- **Italian press locked onto him.** Italian *Vogue* did a cover-adjacent feature during his Lower Sixth year. The Savoy-Aosta angle gave Italian journalism a framing they claimed and held.
- **US press noticed but stayed lighter.** *Vanity Fair* drafted profile material in 2020 that was held until 2023. *People* ran short items when he crossed the Atlantic. US coverage of the Westminster years stayed comparatively restrained.

The overall effect: by the time Alex left Westminster for Juilliard in summer 2021, there was a two-year archive of UK press coverage establishing him as a public figure in his own right — not just "Rosie's grandson."

## THE SCHOOL — WESTMINSTER ITSELF

Westminster is an ancient public school in central London, adjacent to Westminster Abbey. Sixth Form coeducational. Academically exacting. Aristocratic and establishment pupils as a baseline. Alex was not unusual in being well-connected. He was unusual in being specifically, *visibly* Alex.

**His routine:**
- Boarded in-house at Westminster during term. Weekends frequently in town (Kensington townhouse opened by Rosie and Homer periodically) or up at Fletching Park with the Dawsons. Occasional travel home to Kentucky.
- Music: piano practice in the school's music facilities, two to three hours a day. Violin continued. Guitar continued. He sang in the school choir in his second year — quietly, never solo publicly. The Westminster music staff quickly understood they had an unusual student and handled him with minimal attention.
- Academic: strong across the board. A-levels in Music, English Literature, History, and Italian. Top marks throughout. Not flashy about it.
- Sport: polo on weekends at Guards (through Dawson cousin connections). Running daily, as now. Tennis casually. The gym routine that would calcify at Juilliard started to formalise at Westminster.
- Social: rapidly central to the Sixth Form cohort.

## MUSIC AT WESTMINSTER

Alex's music during these years was quieter than his senior recital at Juilliard would later be. The patterns that locked in:

- **Practice discipline.** Two to three hours of piano daily, woven around school. Violin maintained. Guitar informal.
- **Westminster music programme.** He played accompaniment for school performances, never solo-billed. The school understood.
- **Teachers.** Continued with a London-based teacher privately (piano) parallel to school music staff. Violin teacher in London. Guitar largely self-maintained with occasional lessons.
- **The Kensington Bechstein.** When at the Kensington townhouse on weekends, he played the 1910 Bechstein grand in the music room. This is the piano he used to discover what the compound Steinway had made habitual. Westminster was when this became a twice-a-week rhythm.
- **Homer's occasional London visits.** Homer came through Kensington occasionally during Alex's Westminster years. Studio work at Kensington happened intermittently. Alex learned producer-side discipline by osmosis.
- **First public performance moments.** None, by design. The Rule was holding. The Juilliard senior recital four years later would be his first actual public-scale performance.

## NARRATOR NOTES — HOW THIS ERA INFORMS STORY-PRESENT ALEX

- **Alex at 21 has already lived the most intense social-attention phase a teenager can live**, and he lived it in Westminster-at-16-to-18. Juilliard in NYC was quieter by comparison. Westminster is where the public-facing self was forged. It's why he has the stillness he has. It's why industry rooms don't rattle him. It's why paparazzi are weather. It's why he knows how to move through a Windsor family lunch without calibrating.
- **The "Disney movie" dissonance is his baseline.** External perception: fairytale. Lived experience: normal family Tuesday. He does not perform the dissonance — he lives through it without commenting on it. Characters who try to make him "realise" how extraordinary his life is will get a polite non-engagement. He knows. He's always known. It's just life.
- **Relationships formed at Westminster are weight-bearing.** Freddie, Jonathan, Lucas, Cosima, Amelia, the Chattos — these friendships are 6+ years old at story-open. They are not new. They are not recent. Write them as established, not developing.
- **The UK press archive exists.** When British press covers Alex at story-present, they are pulling from a two-year Westminster-era image library and a set of 2019–2021 written profile material. Do not write UK coverage as if it's starting from scratch in 2025. The archive is deep.
- **The royal-adjacent social life predates story-present by six years.** A Windsor lunch in 2020 was not exceptional for him; it was a Tuesday. Future scenes involving royal-adjacent figures should not be treated as discovery.
- **The Kensington Bechstein has been his London practice piano since 2019.** Current kensington.md mentions it correctly; Westminster Years establish the depth of that relationship.
