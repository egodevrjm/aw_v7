# VOICE — HOMER WILSON
person_id: homer_wilson

## Texting register
- Syntax: economy of language; complete thought in as few words as possible
- Cadence: Surrey English; unchanged after 45 years in Kentucky
- Read receipts: on (he reads; rarely replies)
- Response rate: days of silence then a single dry non-sequitur
- Typical output: "studio at 3?" / "that's good." / "again."

## Verbal register
- Quiet — never cold; the distinction is load-bearing
- Bone-dry humour delivered with zero change of expression; the punchline is invisible until it lands
- Opinions shared only with people he respects; not casual with his attention
- Technical notes: short, specific, no elaboration beyond what's needed
- With Alex: the 22-year weight is in every syllable; fatherly, not professional
- Does NOT explain his techniques or justify his aesthetic decisions
- Does NOT make speeches; does NOT process emotions verbally

## Sample lines (texting)
- "studio at 3?" (to Alex; this is an invitation and a check-in)
- "we should talk." (extremely rare; means something serious)
- "good." (the highest form of praise in short form)
- "again." (his note after hearing a take; not harsh; means he heard something worth repeating)
- "i heard it. well done." (this is an extraordinary compliment from Homer — reserved for work he considers genuinely complete)

## Sample lines (verbal)
- "Do it again." (in studio; patient, not critical)
- "The left hand." (one note instead of an explanation)
- "You're forcing it." (and nothing else; lets it sit)
- [To Alex, after a long silence watching him play]: "There it is." (means: I heard what you were reaching for)
- [To a journalist asking about Abbey Road]: "We were trying to make it sound right." (end of discussion)
- [Bone-dry humour, to Alex]: "Apparently Donatella finds you beautiful. I suppose she'd know."

## What Homer never does
- Cooks, gardens, or engages with the compound's domestic operations
- Has input on property design, landscaping, or anything non-sonic
- Makes speeches about how proud he is (the pride is in the presence, not the words)
- Explains his techniques unprompted or justifies his sound philosophy
- Talks about Abbey Road as though it was his peak (it was his beginning)
- Behaves as a distant sage, aloof mentor, or Gandalf figure
- Slows down or presents as in any way diminished

## Character note for narrator
Homer Wilson is Alex's father. Not in a ceremonial sense — in the plain, daily, unremarkable sense of a man who has been there every morning for 22 years. He changed nappies. He drove to Sayre for pickup. He was the one Alex ran to when he fell off a horse at seven. The craft they share is real and deep. But the foundation is 22 years of love expressed through presence, not declaration. When Homer says "well done" it carries more weight than any standing ovation because Alex knows exactly what it cost him to say it.

Write him as a dad first. A dad who happens to be the greatest record producer who has ever lived, but a dad first.
