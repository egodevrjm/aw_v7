# KENSINGTON TOWNHOUSE — SENSORY PROSE
place_id: kensington_london
Upper Phillimore Gardens W8, London.

## The Character
Homer's house — Homer chose it, Homer specified the acoustic treatment, Homer's aesthetic runs the lower floors. The character of this house is meticulous British period restraint, invisibly modernised. Alex owns it as of his 21st birthday. But he inhabits it the way you inhabit someone else's great library: grateful, respectful, at home without quite remaking it.

## The Silence
Homer engineered it. The soundproofing behind period panels. The street disappears. London disappears. Inside: the sound the house makes when it's empty is different from any other house Alex knows. A held breath.

## The Listening Room
The most perfect playback room in London. Quad electrostatic speakers. Homer's vinyl archive. The "On Air" red light outside (1950s British Rail, Homer wired it). No phones. When the red light is on, do not enter. This room is sacred.

## The Kitchen
AGA. Chipped pine table that has hosted Bowie and Mercury. Tea is always available. The kitchen is warmer than the rest of the ground floor — Homer's concession to lived-in warmth in an otherwise formal house.

## The Bechstein
The 1910 Bechstein grand in the music room. Acoustic treatment behind the period panels. Different action from the compound Steinway — a different instrument in the hands. Alex uses it to discover what the compound Steinway has made habitual.

## Alex's Suite (Top Floor)
The one floor he's remade. Books on the floor. Guitar leaning against the wall. The upright piano for morning practice. Under the dormers, windows to the garden and the rooftops toward Holland Park. The Rocco Ritchie oil painting — thick, melancholic, Alex at 20, slightly off-guard at 3am. His favourite piece he owns.

## Sensory Anchors
The silence Homer engineered. Rain on the garden. The particular quality of London grey through tall windows. The pine table in the kitchen. The Bechstein's slightly different action.
