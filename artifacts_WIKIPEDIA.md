**Alex Wilson** (born June 22, 2003) is an American-British pianist. He graduated from The Juilliard School in May 2025 with a Bachelor of Music in Piano Performance, specialising across contemporary, classical, and jazz repertoire, and spent his junior year on exchange at the Royal Academy of Music in London. He is the grandson of singer-songwriter Rosie Walker and record producer and sound engineer Sir Homer Wilson KBE. Video recordings of Wilson's Juilliard senior recital attracted significant international attention upon release.

He serves on the Young Met Board of the Metropolitan Museum of Art.

---

## Early Life

Alexander Wilson was born on June 22, 2003, in Woodford County, Kentucky, to Lady Sarah Anne Dawson Wilson (1981–2003) and David Wilson (1975–2008). His mother, the third child of Richard Dawson, 7th Earl of Fletching, and Margaret Dawson, Countess of Fletching, died shortly after his birth; his father died in 2008. Wilson was subsequently raised by his paternal grandparents, singer-songwriter Rosie Walker and producer-engineer Homer Wilson KBE, who adopted him at birth. He holds dual American and British citizenship.

Wilson was raised at Phoenix Hollow, the Walker-Wilson family estate in Woodford County, Kentucky, which has been associated with significant figures in popular and classical music throughout his grandmother's career.[1] In accordance with the family's long-standing approach to Wilson's minority, he has not given public interviews; biographical details have been communicated through a Walker Holdings biographical fact sheet maintained by SSML Media Relations.[2]

---

## Education

Wilson attended **Sayre School** in Lexington, Kentucky, from kindergarten through Grade 10 (2008–2019), the independent school most closely associated with Woodford County families.[3] He transferred to **Westminster School, London**, for Sixth Form (2019–2021), where he was noted for his performance in the school's music programme.[4]

Wilson was admitted to the **Juilliard School** in New York City on full scholarship, completing a Bachelor of Music in Piano Performance in May 2025. His degree combined training in contemporary, classical, and jazz repertoire — a tri-genre emphasis that has become more common at Juilliard's piano division over the past decade. During his junior year (2023–2024), Wilson undertook an exchange at the **Royal Academy of Music, London**, continuing the same cross-genre focus.

His senior recital at Juilliard, performed in May 2025, attracted significant attention when video recordings were released online.[5] The programme comprised works by Bach, Beethoven, Prokofiev, and Liszt. Juilliard faculty member Dr. Catherine Marlowe described the recital as "the best undergraduate recital in twenty years."[6]

## Music Career

### Juilliard Recital (2025)

Wilson's senior recital at Juilliard, performed in May 2025, attracted significant attention when video recordings were released online following the ceremony. The programme comprised Bach's Chromatic Fantasy & Fugue BWV 903, Beethoven's Sonata No. 31 Op. 110, Prokofiev's Sonata No. 7 Op. 83 ("Stalingrad"), and Liszt's Réminiscences de Don Juan. The recording accumulated several million views within days of release.[6] Juilliard faculty member Dr. Catherine Marlowe described the recital as "the best undergraduate recital in twenty years."[5]

Wilson has not announced any recording deal, album release, or forthcoming commercial performances as of June 2025.

## Public Appearances

Throughout his childhood and adolescence, Wilson accompanied his legal parents to the public cultural events customary for the Walker-Wilson family. In accordance with the Rule (see Fact Sheet, Reference [2]), his attendance was walkthrough-only — Wilson did not give press interviews, did not participate in red-carpet press lines, and did not provide public quotations. His presence at these events is documented photographically across more than a decade of press archives.

### Met Gala

Wilson has attended **The Costume Institute Benefit (the "Met Gala") annually since 2013**, at the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York City. His first attendance, at age 10, wearing custom Versace designed by his godmother Donatella Versace, produced one of the most-circulated Met Gala photographs of the decade and established a pattern of Versace-designed looks that has continued at most subsequent Galas.[25] He has attended twelve consecutive Galas (2013–2025). In 2024, Wilson was appointed to the Young Met Board.[8] His Met Gala arrivals have consistently generated substantial social media engagement and broad fashion-press coverage.

### Awards Ceremonies

Wilson has accompanied his family to the **Grammy Awards annually since childhood**, in association with Rosie Walker. He has also been a regular attendee of the **Academy Awards** and, following his relocation to London for Sixth Form, the **BAFTA Film and Television Awards**. Wilson is a regular guest at the **Elton John AIDS Foundation Academy Awards Party** (Sir Elton John being one of his godparents) and has appeared at the **Kennedy Center Honors** periodically.[26]

### Fashion Weeks

Wilson has attended fashion weeks in New York, London, Milan, and Paris since adolescence, in most cases front-row at a selective set of shows — principally **Versace** (Donatella Versace) and **Stella McCartney** (Stella McCartney, also a godparent), with occasional appearances at other houses. Wilson is not a full-schedule fashion-week attendee; his presence is selective and closely tied to personal connections.[27]

Wilson maintains a largely consistent annual calendar built around fixed summer events that do not conflict with his education. Since his mid-teens, recurring attendance has included:

- **Salzburg Festival, Bayreuth Festival, Edinburgh International Festival** (European summer classical circuit): selective attendance at major European classical-music festivals, most consistently since his Juilliard years.

## Family Background

### Walker-Wilson (paternal)

Wilson was raised by singer-songwriter **Rosie Walker** and producer-engineer **Homer Wilson KBE**, who adopted him at birth following the death of his biological mother, Lady Sarah Anne Dawson Wilson, in 2003. His biological father, David Wilson (1975–2008), was Rosie Walker and Homer Wilson's son. Rosie Walker and Homer Wilson are Wilson's legal parents.

Rosie Walker is widely regarded as one of the most significant figures in twentieth-century popular music, having released twelve studio albums between 1965 and 2021 and won ten Grammy Awards for Album of the Year. Walker's catalog is administered by Walker Publishing and Walker Records, both of which she founded.[12]

Homer Wilson KBE is a record producer and sound engineer who collaborated extensively with Walker and many of the defining artists of the 1960s and 1970s. Wilson was appointed Knight Commander of the Order of the British Empire in 2018 for services to music.[13]

### Dawson (maternal)

Wilson's biological mother, **Lady Sarah Anne Dawson Wilson** (1981–2003), was the third child of **Richard Dawson, 7th Earl of Fletching**, and **Margaret Dawson, Countess of Fletching** (née Whitelock). The Dawson family has held the Earldom of Fletching since its creation in the late seventeenth century; the family seat is **Fletching Park** in East Sussex, a 2,800-acre estate near Haywards Heath that has been in continuous Dawson ownership since the 1690s.[14]

Wilson's maternal grandfather, Richard Dawson, is a City financier and the current Earl of Fletching; the heir apparent to the earldom is Wilson's uncle, **James Dawson, Viscount Hartfield**, founder of Hartfield Capital in London. Wilson has several Dawson cousins, including The Honourable Jonathan Dawson (Westminster School and Oxford) and Lucas Ashby, son of Wilson's aunt Lady Caroline Ashby née Dawson.

Wilson's connection to the Dawson family, long noted in British press coverage of the Walker-Wilson family, became more publicly visible during Wilson's years at Westminster School in London.[15]

## Maternal Royal Ancestry

Wilson is descended from two European royal houses through his maternal great-grandmothers, both of whom married into the British families that produced his grandparents.[16]

### House of Savoy-Aosta (paternal line of his maternal grandfather)

Wilson's paternal great-grandmother on the Dawson side was **HRH Princess Isabella Maria Teresa of Savoy-Aosta** (1918–2005), a member of a cadet branch of the Italian royal House of Savoy-Aosta. Princess Isabella was born in Turin, left Italy during the Second World War, and in 1945 married **Archibald Dawson, 6th Earl of Fletching** (1916–1988). She became Countess of Fletching upon her husband's accession to the earldom. Their son, Richard Dawson, is the current 7th Earl and Wilson's maternal grandfather.[17]

Princess Isabella was a cadet-branch descendant of the Italian royal house that reigned over Italy until the 1946 constitutional referendum which established the Italian Republic. Through this line, Wilson is distantly related to the current heads of the Italian royal houses, including Prince Aimone of Savoy-Aosta.

### House of Glücksburg (maternal line of his maternal grandmother)

Wilson's maternal great-grandmother on the Whitelock-Dawson side was **HRH Princess Anastasia Sophia of Greece and Denmark** (1924–2001), a member of a cadet branch of the Greek royal House of Glücksburg. Princess Anastasia was born in Athens and evacuated to London in 1941 following the German invasion of Greece. She remained in England after the war and in 1948 married **General Sir Edward Whitelock GCB MC** (1912–1998), a British Army officer who had served in North Africa and Italy during the Second World War. Their daughter, Margaret Whitelock, born 1949, later married Richard Dawson, 7th Earl of Fletching, in 1971, becoming Countess of Fletching.[18]

Through the Glücksburg line, Wilson is distantly related to the British royal family via the late Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh (born Prince Philip of Greece and Denmark), as well as to the Danish and Norwegian royal houses and to various other European royal families into which the Glücksburgs married.

### Family and Public Profile

Wilson has been the subject of multiple genealogical and general-interest articles in the British, Italian, and Greek press examining his combined descent from American, British, Italian, and Greek family lines.[19][20] While Wilson has not publicly commented on his royal ancestry, the family has not sought to conceal it; both great-grandmothers are documented in Burke's Peerage & Baronetage entries for the Fletching earldom and associated families.[14]

Wilson is fluent in English, French, Italian, and Spanish. The Walker Holdings fact sheet notes his multilingual fluency as consistent with his family background and early schooling; Wilson himself has not given interviews or made public statements on the subject.[2]

**Rosie Walker** (born Rosa Mae Walker, 1946) is an American singer-songwriter, widely regarded as one of the most significant figures in twentieth-century popular music. Walker has released twelve studio albums between 1965 and 2021, winning ten Grammy Awards for Album of the Year — more than any other artist in the award's history. In 1980, she ceased touring and public performance and has since declined all press engagements, a withdrawal from the commercial apparatus of the music industry that has been referred to in music journalism as "The Withdrawal."[1] She has continued to release studio albums at irregular intervals since 1995.

Walker founded Walker Records and Walker Publishing, through which her catalog is administered. She remains active as a businessperson through Walker Holdings, the broader family enterprise she established in Woodford County, Kentucky.

---

## Career

Walker's active performing career spanned fifteen years, from her debut in 1965 through her final tour in 1979. Her work bridged the folk, country, and singer-songwriter traditions of the 1960s and 1970s, winning Grammy Awards for Album of the Year for six of her first eight albums — a record that has never been approached by any other artist.

Her live performances, particularly during the late 1960s and 1970s, were considered landmark events; footage and recordings from this period remain widely circulated. In 1980, Walker ceased performing publicly and has declined all press engagements since that year. She has given no interviews and made no public appearances in a performing capacity since her final concert on December 31, 1979.

Walker returned to recording in 1995 with *Glass Garden*, her first studio album in sixteen years. Subsequent releases — *A House With No Mirrors* (2003), *Black Sun Archive* (2008), and *The Quiet Index* (2021) — each arrived without accompanying press, interviews, or live performance. All four won the Grammy Award for Album of the Year, bringing her total to ten — a record that is not expected to be surpassed.

Walker has declined all offers to re-enter public performance, including a reported request to appear at a major international festival in 2018 that was described by sources close to the family as "not even considered."[2]

---

## Business and Family

Walker founded Walker Records and Walker Publishing to control her catalog and publishing rights during a period — the 1970s — when such independence was rare for any artist, and virtually unprecedented for a woman working in country and folk music.[3]

Walker Holdings, the broader family enterprise, operates through several subsidiaries including Hatfield Group, which runs Hatfield Bar & Grill locations across the United States and United Kingdom, as well as distillery, boutique hotel, and private members' club operations.

Walker is married to Homer Wilson KBE, the record producer and sound engineer with whom she collaborated throughout her recording career. Their daughter, Sarah Dawson Wilson, died in 2003. Alex Wilson, the classical pianist, is her grandson, whom she raised at the family's estate in Woodford County, Kentucky.

---

## Phoenix Hollow

The Walker-Wilson family estate, Phoenix Hollow, in Woodford County, Kentucky, has been associated with significant figures in popular and classical music throughout Walker's career and beyond. The estate contains a private recording studio used by Homer Wilson for production work. Walker has lived there since purchasing the property in the late 1960s and has rarely been photographed there.[4]

**Sir Homer Wilson KBE** (born 1944) is a British record producer and sound engineer. He is widely regarded as one of the defining technical and creative figures of the popular music recording era, having worked with numerous significant artists across the 1960s, 1970s, and beyond. Wilson was appointed Knight Commander of the Order of the British Empire in 2018 for services to music.[1]

Wilson is married to American singer-songwriter Rosie Walker, with whom he collaborated throughout her recording career. He continues to work as a producer and engineer from a private facility at the Walker-Wilson family estate in Woodford County, Kentucky, and periodically from London.

---

## Career

Wilson began his career as a studio engineer in London in the early 1960s, during a period of significant technical and creative innovation in British recording studios. His distinctive approach to capturing acoustic instruments — particularly piano and voice — became a recognised element of his work across multiple decades and genres.[2]

He produced and engineered Rosie Walker's entire studio discography and has been credited as an engineer, co-producer, or technical consultant on numerous other significant recordings of the period. Wilson has spoken infrequently about his work in interviews, preferring to discuss technique rather than reputation: "The room hears the music first. My job is to not get in the way of that."[3]

Wilson was appointed KBE in the 2018 New Year Honours for services to music, a recognition that prompted a rare public statement from the family.

---

## Working Method

Wilson is known for analogue recording techniques developed across decades of practice. He has been resistant to aspects of digital production he considers disadvantageous to acoustic performance, though he works with digital tools where appropriate. Several prominent musicians and producers have made what music journalists have described as "pilgrimage visits" to the Phoenix Hollow facility to work with Wilson or simply to observe his methods.[4]

He continues to take limited production projects on a selective basis from the Phoenix Hollow studio.
